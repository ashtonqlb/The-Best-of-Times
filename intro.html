Introduction

Overview

This guide will instruct you on how to properly install Tale of Two Wastelands - a total conversion mod for Fallout: New Vegas that merges the games of Fallout 3 and New Vegas together!

This 'Mirelurked' version of the guide modifies the pre-installation section specifically for SteamOS 3 handhelds like the Steam Deck or Lenovo Legion Go S.

The guide is highly accessible to everyone, no matter your modding experience. It is expected for you to be relatively experienced with operating a computer in general.

As there are many small instructions that are vital for your game to function, it is incredibly important that you read all the instructions very carefully, even if you believe you are experienced enough to skip them.

Requirements

  • English copies of both Fallout 3 - Game of the Year Edition and Fallout: New Vegas - Ultimate Edtion on Steam.
    • You may also use standard editions of Fallout 3 and Fallout: New Vegas as long as you own all of the DLC.
    • GOG, and Epic Games versions of the games can be installed with Heroic Games Launcher but are not supported by this guide. Caveat emptor.
    • The Microsoft Store versions are not supported. Furthermore, there is no Microsoft Store on Linux
    • The Games for Windows Live version of Fallout 3 is supported as long as it's the Game of The Year Edition and in English.
    • The Polish-Czech-Russian (PCR) version of Fallout: New Vegas is fully supported as long as the game itself is in English.
    • The German release uses a "No Gore" version, making it also incompatible with xNVSE. If you have it on Steam, you can use DepotDownloader to download the international EXE.
  • A method of connecting your handheld device to a keyboard, mouse & monitor. Such as a dock or a software KVM like Barrier.
  • At least 40GB of free internal storage space, both games included. Tale of Two Wastelands itself is 17GB.
  • SteamOS 3.7.8 or higher.

    • SteamOS 3 copycat distributions deviating from the public release (e.g. ChimeraOS or Bazzite) are not supported by us. We can and will refuse to provide help in such cases.
    This is because they don't guarantee the same functionality expected from the official SteamOS, which can cause unforeseen issues.

  • A sudo password set on your SteamOS device.
  • A Nexus Mods account (a Premium subscription will allow uncapped download speeds).
  • A MOD:PUB account.
